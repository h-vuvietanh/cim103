Toby Fox

Toby Fox, real name Robert F. Fox, is an American musician and game developer. He was born on October 11, 1991 in Manchester, New Hampshire and had an interest in music creation ever since he was a kid. Toby is an alumnus of Northeastern University's class of 2014 and held a degree in environmental science.

Inspiration and Early Works

Toby fell in love with role-playing games thanks to his experience with Final Fantasy VI and several Japanese works, most notably EarthBound, Secret of Mana, Super Mario RPG, and Touhou Project. These inspirations can be seen in his style of composing and the video games he later developed.

Fox's most notable programming creation in his teenage years is a Halloween-themed ROM hack of the 2008 video game Earthbound, aptly named EarthBound Halloween Hack. It is here that the earliest draft of his most famous song, Megalovania, first appeared. He later reused this song for the multimedia webcomic Homestuck. Toby's musical contributions to Homestuck has helped him gain some traction among the online community as a talented composer. During this period, he worked under the alias Radiation / FwugRadiation.

Undertale and Deltarune

Undertale, a meta-fictional video game released in 2015, is Toby Fox's most influential and well-known work. The game was mostly developed by Toby and the artist Temmie Chang, and they took some inspiration from earlier-mentioned works, such as the bullet hell combat system of Touhou Project and the atmosphere of EarthBound. The game's success can be attributed to its unique, charming cast of characters, how Undertale went against many pre-established tropes of the role-playing game genre, and the way game mechanics intertwined itself with the story. The internet was obsessed with Undertale, creating a huge fanbase with many derivative works whose popularity rivals the original game itself. Toby did not anticipate the pop-culture phenomenon his creation would create, and initially remarked the attention as overwhelming: Not only did I not expect this level of popularity, but initially, I was afraid of it.

Three years later, Toby Fox released the first chapter of Deltarune -- another role-playing game featuring some characters from Undertale and can be considered its spinoff. Deltarune was created in an episodic nature, meaning that chapters of the game would be released occasionally over the course of several years. While it was initially overshadowed by the predecessor Undertale, the game gradually established its own identity with the release of Chapter 2 in 2021 and Chapter 3 + 4 in 2025, with arguably better artworks and storytelling than what was seen in Toby's older works. Fox has remarked that Deltarune is his dream creation and Undertale, despite its huge success and impact, was intended to be an experiment to see whether or not his ambition can become true.
